Education & Learnig Experience

My name is Azelarab Essyagh, and I am a Computer Science student at Weifang University of Science and Technology. Choosing computer science was an important decision because I have always been interested in technology and how computers work. I wanted to understand how software is created and how websites and applications are developed. Since I started studying this major, I have learned many new skills that have helped me improve both academically and personally.

During my university studies, I have taken different programming courses that introduced me to several programming languages, including C, Python, HTML, and CSS. At first, programming was not easy because many concepts were completely new to me. I had to learn about variables, loops, conditions, functions, arrays, and many other topics. Sometimes I spent a long time trying to solve one problem, but every challenge helped me understand programming better. With regular practice, I became more confident and started solving problems more quickly.

One of the courses that I enjoyed the most was Web Programming. In this course, I learned how to build websites using HTML and CSS. I learned how to create headings, paragraphs, tables, forms, images, hyperlinks, lists, and navigation menus. After learning HTML, I studied CSS to make webpages more attractive by adding colors, changing fonts, creating layouts, and improving the overall design. It was exciting to see a simple webpage become a complete website after applying different styles.

Another important course was C Programming. This course helped me understand the basic principles of programming and problem solving. I learned how to write programs using variables, data types, loops, functions, arrays, pointers, and file handling. These topics improved my logical thinking and taught me how to break a difficult problem into smaller and simpler steps. Although some exercises were difficult, completing them gave me a better understanding of programming and increased my confidence.

Besides attending classes, I also practiced programming outside the classroom. I used HackerRank to solve programming challenges and improve my coding skills. Each exercise gave me a chance to practice what I had learned during lectures. Solving different problems helped me become more familiar with writing efficient code and understanding different programming techniques. I also earned certificates and badges, which motivated me to continue learning and improving my skills.

I also used SoloLearn as another learning platform. It provided interactive lessons, quizzes, and coding exercises that helped me review programming concepts at my own pace. The lessons were easy to follow, and the quizzes helped me remember important information. Whenever I finished a topic, I felt more confident about using it in my university assignments. Learning from different resources allowed me to strengthen my understanding of programming and develop better study habits.

During my studies, I learned that programming requires patience and continuous practice. Sometimes my code did not work because of very small mistakes, such as missing punctuation or incorrect file names. Instead of giving up, I learned how to read error messages, test my programs carefully, and fix problems step by step. This experience taught me that mistakes are a normal part of learning and that every error is an opportunity to improve.

University life has also helped me develop important personal skills. Working on projects and assignments improved my time management, communication, and teamwork. I learned how to organize my work, meet deadlines, and cooperate with classmates when completing group activities. These experiences helped me become more responsible and prepared me for future professional work.

Looking ahead, I want to continue improving my programming knowledge and learn more advanced technologies such as JavaScript, databases, and modern web development frameworks. My goal is to become a professional software developer who can build useful applications and websites that solve real-world problems. I believe that learning never stops, and I will continue practicing, exploring new technologies, and improving my skills throughout my career. My education has given me a strong foundation, and I am excited to keep growing as a computer science student and future software engineer.
